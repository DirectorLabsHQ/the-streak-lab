30-Day No Drinking Experiment

A simple, time-boxed reset: zero alcohol for 30 days. Observe what changes in sleep, energy, mood, focus, skin, or habits.

Goal

Test how removing alcohol affects your daily experience — without pressure to continue forever.

Rules

  • Win: No alcoholic beverages (beer, wine, spirits, cocktails, etc.) for the full calendar day.
  • Does not count: Non-alcoholic beer/wine (0.0%), mocktails without alcohol.
  • Minimum viable day: If socializing, bring or order a clear non-alcoholic alternative (sparkling water + lime, NA beer, etc.).
  • Start date: Any day — no need to wait for January 1.
  • Duration: 30 days (extend or stop early if it makes sense).
Important: If you drink heavily or daily, consult a doctor before stopping suddenly — abrupt changes can be unsafe for some people.

Note for Day

Common Timeline (from participant reports & studies)

  • Days 1–3: Possible mild withdrawal (headache, irritability, cravings) if habitual drinker. Hydration & sleep help.
  • Days 4–7: Sleep often improves (deeper, fewer interruptions), morning energy up.
  • Days 8–14: Skin clearer, less bloating, steadier mood, potential small weight drop (fewer empty calories).
  • Days 15–30: Many report sharper focus, better digestion, lower inflammation markers. Habit control strengthens.

Results vary widely — this is personal data collection, not guaranteed outcomes.

Streak Math

  • Danger zone: Days 7–14 — most slips happen here (social events, weekends).
  • Miss tolerance: One slip → ~60–70% chance of finishing strong if you restart immediately.
  • Calorie math example: Cutting 150–300 kcal/day from drinks → potential 0.5–2 kg difference over 30 days (varies by person).
